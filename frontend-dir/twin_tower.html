Introduction to the Twin Towers (Hung Thạnh Towers)

The Twin Towers, also known as Hung Thạnh Towers, are located on Tran Hung Dao Street, Dong Da Ward, Quy Nhơn City, Binh Dinh Province. According to records preserved by the Management Board of the Twin Towers Heritage Site, these towers date back to the late 12th to early 13th century.

Among the remaining Cham towers in Binh Dinh, the Twin Towers stand out not only for their proximity to the sea — just about 4 km southeast from the coastline — but also for their architectural uniqueness. The complex consists of two adjacent towers, with one particularly distinctive structure that showcases the quintessential artistry of Cham architecture in the region. In 1980, the Twin Towers were recognized by the Ministry of Culture and Information as a National Architectural Art Monument.

How to Get to the Twin Towers in Quy Nhơn

The Twin Towers are located close to the city center of Quy Nhơn, making them easily accessible. If you're traveling from downtown Quy Nhơn, follow this suggested route: Start from the Vo Nguyen Giap roundabout, head onto Dong Da Street, then continue onto Tran Hung Dao Street. Keep going straight along Tran Hung Dao until you reach a T-junction — turn right and follow the road straight ahead. You’ll soon arrive at the Twin Towers of Quy Nhơn.

What Makes the Twin Towers in Quy Nhơn Special?

3.1 Unique Architectural Art

The Twin Towers were constructed with a distinctive architectural design. Both towers feature a main entrance facing south and are built using fired bricks, tightly stacked and bound together with a special adhesive—a construction technique that remains a mystery to this day.

The larger tower stands about 25 meters high, with elegant fluting and symmetrical floral patterns, and features a meditating monk flanked by elephant figures. The smaller tower, at 23 meters, shows playful depictions of a herd of 13 deer in various poses.

3.2 Discovery of Precious Artifacts

Although few artifacts remain and many are incomplete, each is a valuable relic of the ancient Champa civilization. These include finely crafted sculptures depicting elephants, mythical birds, dancing women, a dragon relief, a column with inscriptions, and the head of the sacred Nandin bull—all on display at the Binh Dinh Museum.

3.3 A Place of Cultural and Spiritual Significance

The Hung Thạnh Twin Towers are not only a remarkable example of Cham architecture but also serve as a cultural beacon that preserves and shares the spiritual heritage of ancient Champa. Visitors can enjoy traditional Cham dance performances at the site, which re-enact age-old rituals and bring the rich cultural legacy to life.

Important Tips When Visiting Twin Towers

If you’re planning a visit, keep in mind the following:

  • Opening Hours: 7:00 AM - 6:00 PM (daily).
  • Ticket Price: 20,000 VND per person. Discounts apply for children (6–15 years), students, research groups, and senior citizens over 60 (with ID).
  • Visitor Regulations:
    • No flammable materials, explosives, or weapons are allowed.
    • Avoid superstitious activities and respect the sanctity of the heritage site.
    • Do not litter, deface, vandalize, or excavate the area.
    • Contact the Binh Dinh Museum for guided tours and further information.
